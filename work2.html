  • Statement:

    Memories of Cars reflects on the streams of traffic which are ever present on large highways that flow and merge in a huge display of human cooperation and mechanical triumph. One’s own body becomes inseparable from the car as do the cars and bodies of the people around them. We communicate with our new bodies, saying that I will move left, that I am stopping, that I’ll let you in, that I’ll keep you out. These car bodies are marketed to us as symbols of our sexuality, our social status, our individualism, and our desire to surpass human limits. They are bonded to us by our desires and their perceived ability to fulfill them. These memories of cars in the way we know them are incredibly specific to the time and place in history we live in, a memory of temporary reality shared by our generations. They are so interwoven into our lives and personhood they are often invisible.

    It is not until the system of cars breaks down or changes that we notice it. Traffic accidents interrupt the flowing stream of vehicles and make us violently aware how different we are from the car bodies we have adopted. Approaching self-driving car technologies make us feel threatened in our car-autonomy. The dinosaur bones our cars consume are limited in supply, and their consumption contributes to a temperature change in the whole planet. The daily flow of cars through our lives is beautiful and a strong memory we will hold of this time in human history, but this flow also holds underneath it at all times inevitable breakdown.
